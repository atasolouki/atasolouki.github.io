About Me

Article writer with 10 years of experience

I've completed both undergraduate and graduate in electrical engineering in the fieled of control theory at the top 5 universities in Iran. I have an experience of working as a data scientist at Part AI research center (the biggest AI research center in Iran). I also keep my connection to the university, and I re-joined the intelligent control system laboratory (ICSLab) as a researcher at Tarbiat Modares University to work on SLAM for capsule endoscopic robots sponsored by the vice president of the science and technology of Iran Since I was very eager in computer vision, I chose my undergraduate and graduate thesis in this field. My undergraduate thesis was about the pose estimation of quadrotors by applying computer vision techniques. My master thesis was "simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM) quadrupedal robot by an RGB-D camera", during this project, I got familiar with photogrammetry techniques. Also, I worked with point clouds since the mapping section of the project was about the 3D reconstruction of the environment. I also have done several projects in computer vision during my education, such as implementing "2 DoF moving object tracker", which was implemented on Raspberry pi board, and implementing "multi moving object tracker in a very low frame rate camera" by applying Kalman Filter. It was late in my undergraduate period when I got familiar with artificial intelligence, so I took some courses like "intelligent control systems," which focused on designing control systems using neural networks. Also, I passed "fuzzy control systems". I was the teaching assistant of these two courses for several semesters. I was keeping my study, and I became skillful in this field in a way that I was a teacher assistant of deep learning last semester at Tarbiat Modares University. The course was held for the first time in the control engineering group, and I was in charge of compiling the course titles. Currently, I am a doctoral research student at Ghent University. I am working on an AR application for wrist surgery as my thesis. To accomplish this, we strive to incorporate point-cloud registration, human mesh recovery (HMR), object tracking, augmented reality, and, of course, real-time integration
